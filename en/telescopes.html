Telescopes

Observing is one of my favorite parts of astronomy, from planning and collecting the data to reducing and analyzing it.

My research has involved a broad range of facilities and techniques, including optical imaging and spectroscopy, infrared spectroscopy, and radio interferometry. This page highlights some of the telescopes and observatories I have used in my work, along with a few that I have had the opportunity to visit.

Dark Energy Camera (DECam)
Víctor M. Blanco Telescope (4 m), Cerro Tololo Inter-American Observatory, La Serena, Chile.

Very Large Array (VLA)
Socorro, New Mexico, USA.

Magellan Baade Telescope
6.5 m, Las Campanas Observatory, La Serena, Chile.

UTP Observatory
12-inch telescope, Universidad Tecnológica de Pereira, Pereira, Colombia.

Atacama Large Millimeter/submillimeter Array (ALMA)
San Pedro de Atacama, Chile.

MDM Observatory
2.4 m telescope, Kitt Peak Observatory, Tucson, Arizona, USA.

Very Large Telescope (VLT)
Paranal Observatory, Antofagasta, Chile.

San Pedro Mártir Observatory
2 m telescope, Ensenada, Mexico.
